

The \phis measurement of \equref{} was perfomed under the assumption that the feynaman diagram responsible for the \BsJpsiPhi decay
is the tree decay diagram of \figref{}. However the same decay could also take place via the so called {\it penguin} diagram, or topology, of \figref{}(b).
Potential contribution of the last could alter the Standard Model expectation value of \phis as shown in \equref{}. So far the experimental
precition was lower than the maximum shift that the additional pengiun topology could induce. However this is not the case for future \phis
measurements as controling those penguin topology contributions becomas mandatory. This issue is the main focus of the current thesis and
the necessary introduction is presented in the current section.
